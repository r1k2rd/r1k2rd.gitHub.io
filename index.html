should i pick up cycling?
About Save money Get fit

Feel like retanking your car and increasing fuel costs are really draining you wallet each month? In that case cycling might be something worth considering.

Whether you're going to work or school every day and back, it really amounts to a great deal of distance over time that equals less money spent on filling the gas tank. Not to mention all the repair costs of maintaining a driveable car. A bicycle on the other hand is less demanding in terms of maintenance costs and only needs the power of thighs to get rolling.

Biking is an awesome way to burn calories and get lean. Even if you use your bicycle only for 30min per day to get to your destination and back home, if you think about it, those minutes amount to a lot of time if you do it every day. The amount of calories burned depends on a few things though: roughness of your local streets, condition of your bicycle, altitude change etc. For example you will have to pedal harder with empty tyres and on a sandy road compared to an asphalt or a concrete road. Riding downhill doesn't really burn many calories because gravity is doing the work for you but going uphill can burn a lot of calories because you will be working against the gravity and believe me: when you feel your thighs burn on top of that hill, you will know that now you can skip a leg day if you are a gym person. Taking on a sprinting challenge or an uphill challenge will make your legs do anaerobic work that gives your legs a really well rounded workout, try for yourself!
